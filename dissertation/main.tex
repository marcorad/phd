\begin{abstract}
    
In this dissertation by publication, we investigate the application of wavelet scattering transforms on whale vocalisations, by following the natural progression of 3 consecutive papers.

We consider how wavelets can be used to replace the short-time Fourier transform (STFT) in signal detection. Wavelet scattering is introduced to the field of passive acoustic monitoring as a convolutional neural network (CNN) alternative for small datasets, which is combined with an improved entropy-based signal detector.

For 1-dimensional (1D) signals, wavelet scattering can be considered as a generalisation of Mel-frequency cepstral coefficients (MFCCs), and can typically outperform MFCCs in audio classification applications. In general, wavelet scattering filtering operations resemble the first layers of a CNN. An effective feature extractor may be constructed with wavelet scattering filters without requiring any learning. The main contribution of this dissertation is the introduction of separable filters for a wavelet scattering transform, which is generalised to an arbitrary number of dimensions.


    
\end{abstract}

\begin{abstract}[afrikaans]
    
  In hierdie publikasie proefskrif, ondersoek ons die toepassing van wavelet scattering transformasies op walvis vokaliserings, deur middel van opeenvolgende joernaal arikels.

  Ons orweeg hoe wavelets gebruik kan word om die kort-tyd Fourier transformasie (KTFT) in sein deteksie te vervang. Wavelet scattering is bekendgestel in die veld van passiewe akoustiese monitering, as an alternatief to konvolusionele neurale netwerke (KNNs). Verder, kombineer ons wavelet scattering met 'n klassieke entropie-gebasseerde sein detektor wat ons verbeter met addisionele tegnieke.

  Vir eendimensionele seine kan wavelet scattering as 'n veralgemening van Mel-frekwensie kepstrale koeffisi\"{e}nte (MFKKs) beskou word, altans 'n verbeterde weergawe daarvan. In die algemeen, is wavelet scattering vergelykbaar met die eerste lae van KNNs. Die hoof bydrag van hierdie proefskrif is die bekendstelling van skeibare filters vir die wavelet scattering transform, wat veralgemeen is vir 'n arbitr\^{e}re aantal intreedimensies.
    
\end{abstract}
